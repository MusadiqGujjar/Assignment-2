Business & Technology

Business technology is any electronic object or system that helps employees
accomplish tasks. The technology that employees use includes computers,
internet systems, printers, mobile devices and software applications that help
them organize and prioritize work. Business technology can also help divide
work as well, giving employees different goals to make larger tasks easier.
Managers can use business technology to look for new hires, arrange
collaborations with other businesses or assess the company's finance.

Technology has an important place in almost every business function.
Whether you're communicating with your team, updating a policy
or reviewing old records, technology has a major part in helping employees
complete jobs and progress in their careers. Learning more about business
technology and how it affects the workplace can help you better use what's
available in your workplace to your advantage. In this article, we review what
business technology is, explore different technology types and explain their
advantages.

Types of Business & Technology

  1. Computer Systems:

    Computer systems are very useful pieces of technology that help support
    almost all the others in a modern business system. It enables employees with
    software tools that allow them to write letters, send emails, analyze records
    and design presentations. Computers can be portable, allowing employees to
    accomplish tasks even as they travel. A computer can help employees work
    from home with software that gives them monitoring systems, cameras, work
    tools and communication programs. Most people in a modern workplace use a
    computer, even if they share it with other employees.

  2. Productivity Tools:

    Computer software and productivity tools help employees accomplish tasks
    and stay focused on their work. These software tools allow employees to edit
    documents, process code, arrange spreadsheets and complete instruction
    forms. Some productivity tools help employees schedule their work,
    suggesting when to work and when to take a short break.

  3. Mail and phone systems:

    Both mail and phone systems help employees communicate with one another
    about daily tasks and projects. Businesses commonly use in-line phones that
    only work on the company premises, although more modern establishments
    may give leaders company administered cellphones. All phone systems in a
    company usually have auto attendants that help callers find the employee
    they want to reach. Work phone systems may also include custom voicemail
    options as well, for when employees are away from work. Email systems work
    similarly to company phone systems, usually being exclusive to the company
